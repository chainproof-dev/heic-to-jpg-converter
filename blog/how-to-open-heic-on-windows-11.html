How to open HEIC on Windows 11

Feb 8, 2026 8 min read Umar Pathan
Quick Answer: To open HEIC files on Windows 11 for free, search the Microsoft Store for "HEVC Video Extensions from Device Manufacturer" (not the $0.99 version). Install it alongside "HEIF Image Extensions" for full native support. Alternatively, use our free online HEIC to JPG converter that processes files locally in your browser.

In 2026, the High Efficiency Image Coding (HEIC) format has moved beyond the Apple ecosystem and is now a standard across premium Android 16 devices and high-end mirrorless cameras. However, Windows 11 users are still frequently met with the "HEVC Video Extension is required" prompt, often asking for a $0.99 payment to view their own photos.

If you are "stuck in HEIC hell," you don't need to pay the fee or risk your privacy with shady online uploaders. This guide covers the most effective, SEO-backed methods to open and convert HEIC files for free while keeping your metadata intact.

Why Windows 11 Still Struggles with HEIC in 2026

HEIC files are containers based on the HEVC (H.265) video codec. Because this codec is proprietary and subject to complex patent licensing, Microsoft does not include it by default in Windows 11. This "compatibility gap" is the primary reason users encounter errors when trying to open iPhone or Samsung UltraHDR photos in the native Photos app.

Method 1: The "Manufacturer" Loophole (Free Official Extension)

The most common mistake is following the direct prompt to the paid extension in the Microsoft Store. Instead, you can often find the "HEVC Video Extensions from Device Manufacturer" for free.

1

Install Free HEVC Extension

  1. Close any open image apps.
  2. Search the Microsoft Store for the full name: "HEVC Video Extensions from Device Manufacturer".
  3. Alternatively, use the system link: ms-windows-store://pdp/?ProductId=9n4wgh0z6vhq
  4. Install the HEIF Image Extensions alongside it to ensure full OS integration.

Troubleshooting Common HEIC Bugs

The "Black and White" Glitch

Some users report that updated encoders turn colored HEIC files into black and white JPEGs. This is often a codec conflict; try rolling back your HEVC extension to a previous version if this occurs.

  • Sideways Images: If your converted JPEGs are oriented incorrectly, it's because the converter failed to read the Orientation Tag in the metadata. Tools like iMazing or libheif are better at maintaining this tag.
  • Blurry Results: To avoid quality loss, ensure your converter is set to a "Quality" of 92% or at least 85%. HEIC is 10-bit while JPEG is 8-bit, so some data loss is inevitable, but high-quality settings minimize the visual impact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the HEVC extension from Device Manufacturer really free?

Yes, the extension titled "HEVC Video Extensions from Device Manufacturer" is completely free, unlike the standard HEVC extension which costs $0.99. The free version is typically pre-licensed for devices from major manufacturers.

Why do my converted JPEGs appear sideways?

This happens when the converter fails to read the EXIF Orientation Tag embedded in the image. Tools like iMazing, libheif, and our online converter properly preserve and apply this metadata during conversion.

Does converting HEIC to JPG lose quality?

Some quality loss is inevitable since HEIC supports 10-bit color depth while JPEG is limited to 8-bit. However, by setting your converter quality to 92-95%, visual degradation is minimal and usually imperceptible to the human eye.

How can I stop taking HEIC photos on iPhone?

Open the Settings app, tap Camera, then Formats, and select "Most Compatible". This will capture photos as JPEGs directly, avoiding the need for any conversion.
